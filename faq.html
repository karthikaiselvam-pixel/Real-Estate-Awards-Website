World Real Estate Excellence Awards Frequently Asked Questions Everything You Need To Know

Everything you need to know about entering, judging, deadlines, fees, the gala and what winners receive.

Programme Overview

An international, jury-led recognition programme honouring outstanding property developers, architects, brokers, agencies, interior designers and luxury property brands across 100+ countries.
The programme is delivered by Golden Tree Awards, an independent international awards organiser headquartered in Dubai. The awards secretariat is based in Business Bay, Dubai.
Since 2019. More than 450 organisations and projects have been honoured across the programme's history.
35+ categories across five regions and 100+ countries, spanning developer, architecture, broker, agency, luxury, smart, sustainable and specialist tracks.
Yes. The jury is independent, declared conflicts are recused, and scoring criteria are published in advance.

Eligibility & Entry

Property developers, architects, brokers, agencies, construction firms, interior designers, smart-building specialists, property managers and proptech innovators of any size across any region.
Yes. Sub-categories ensure boutique organisations compete fairly with larger international brands.
Yes. International developers, architects and brokers actively operating in a region are eligible with judging context appropriate to their footprint.
There is no hard cap. Most entrants compete in two to four categories; multi-category entries qualify for discounted fees.
Entry fees vary by category and entry window — early-bird (discounted), standard, and late-entry (premium). Specific pricing is on the official entry portal.

Judging & Criteria

Independently by a senior global jury against published criteria specific to each category. The jury chair publishes a yearly methodology note.
Yes. Category-specific weightings are published in the resources section and alongside each category's detailed criteria.
Through verified performance — embodied carbon, operational energy, water, biodiversity — and credible third-party certification. Marketing claims without supporting evidence score poorly.
Eligibility review ~2 weeks after entry close; jury scoring ~4–6 weeks; shortlists ~4–6 weeks before the gala; winners revealed live at the gala.
Eligibility queries can be raised during eligibility review. Final jury scoring decisions are not subject to challenge to preserve independence.

Winners & Recognition

A globally recognised physical trophy, a digital and print winner's seal with usage guidelines, an editorial profile in the winners directory, press release distribution and alumni network access.
Permanent. Winners can use the seal indefinitely. Year-specific recognition (e.g. '2026 Winner') is particularly impactful in the year following.
Yes. Detailed digital and print usage guidelines accompany the winner's seal.
Yes. A single project can win at country, regional and global level with separate jury scoring at each stage.
Each year in November in Dubai. Specific dates are published on the official entry portal.

Process & Submission

Online via the official entry portal. 20–30 minutes per category.
Written submission, photography, drawings (where applicable), performance data and supporting documentation. Specifics vary by category.
JPG, PNG, PDF, MP4. Maximum file sizes are published on the entry portal.
