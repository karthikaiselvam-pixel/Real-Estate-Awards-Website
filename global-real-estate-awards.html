The Global Real Estate Story In 2026

Real estate is the world's largest asset class — and the most consequential built environment shaping how we live, work, learn, gather and travel. The Global Real Estate Awards honour the people and organisations producing the most exceptional work in that industry, across every major region of the world.

The 2026 programme spans 90+ countries, seven regions and 35+ award categories. Entries come from master developers shaping new cities, residential builders delivering iconic projects, architectural practices producing buildings that define a generation, brokers and agencies redefining customer experience, interior designers reimagining luxury living, construction firms delivering technical excellence and proptech innovators reshaping how real estate is built, sold and operated.

The Global Real Estate Awards function as a credibility ladder. Recognition at country level builds towards regional level recognition; regional recognition builds towards continental and global recognition. The result is the most comprehensive and credible property recognition framework in the industry.

The Global Programme Structure

The programme runs across seven regions — Asia, Europe, Middle East, North America, South America, Africa and Oceania — each with dedicated regional jury, regional categories and country-specific recognition. The Luxury Property Awards Worldwide and the Global Real Estate Awards top-of-funnel categories sit above the regional programmes, with explicitly global recognition.

Categories span the full real estate value chain. Developer recognition includes Developer Of The Year, Master Developer Of The Year, Residential Developer Of The Year, Commercial Developer Of The Year, Mixed-Use Developer Of The Year, Luxury Developer Of The Year, Sustainable Developer Of The Year and Affordable Housing Developer Of The Year. Architecture recognition spans residential, commercial, hospitality, civic and adaptive-reuse work. Broker and agency recognition spans network, agency, team and individual broker level.

Why The World's Leading Real Estate Brands Compete For Recognition

Recognition matters because the buyer, partner, lender and talent decisions in real estate are credibility-driven. Institutional investors evaluating developer track-record, HNW buyers shortlisting luxury agencies, multinational tenants choosing commercial landlords, lenders pricing risk and engineers choosing employers all read independent third-party recognition as a primary credibility signal.

The Global Real Estate Awards are designed for that role. Built on an independent jury, transparent criteria, regional context and a 90+ country footprint, the programme produces recognition that resonates across every commercial channel — marketing, sales, PR, investor relations, lender conversations, partnership development and talent attraction.

Past winners consistently report measurable commercial impact — stronger inbound demand, faster sales cycles, higher conversion in international markets, improved competition shortlist rates and stronger inbound recruitment. The award is not a marketing trophy; it is a credibility multiplier with defined commercial outcomes.

Approaching A Global Awards Entry

Strong entries lead with the distinctive idea, follow with quantified outcomes and present design, performance and impact in a structure the jury can score against published criteria. Generic positioning language consistently underperforms specific, well-quantified narrative.

We recommend entering between two and four categories — typically a mix of corporate-level (Developer Of The Year, Luxury Developer Of The Year, Sustainable Developer Of The Year) and project-level (Luxury Property Of The Year, Architecture Excellence, Smart Building Of The Year) recognition. Multi-category entries qualify for discounted fees and increase the probability of winning at least one category.

  • Lead with the work's most distinctive idea or quality.
  • Quantify outcomes — sales velocity, occupancy, certification, embodied-carbon performance.
  • Address the global context — where this work sits relative to international peers.
  • Submit independent press, third-party data and case studies.
  • Use high-quality photography — the awards' archive is shared with international press.
  • Submit two weeks ahead of deadline.

The Global Jury And Judging Framework

The Global Real Estate Awards jury includes senior international developers, master architects, real estate fund executives, urban planners and academic experts. Jurors declare conflicts and recuse themselves from any entry where independence cannot be guaranteed.

Scoring uses a published weighting framework that varies by category. Developer awards weight financial performance, delivery, customer outcomes and partnership credibility. Architecture awards weight design quality, technical resolution and innovation. Sustainability awards weight measurable performance and credible certification. Broker and agency awards weight transaction outcomes, customer satisfaction and brand authority.

Shortlists are reviewed by a chair-led panel before announcement. Shortlisted entrants are notified ahead of the gala and receive recognition assets reflecting their placement, while final winners are revealed live on the night.

Trends Shaping The 2026 Global Awards

Three forces are shaping award-grade global real estate in 2026. The first is operational real estate — institutional capital is moving rapidly into build-to-rent, student housing, senior living, data centres and life-sciences across every major region. The developer category set has expanded to reflect this. The second is decarbonisation — embodied-carbon disclosure and verified low-carbon material strategies are now standard expectations in award-grade new product.

The third is service and operation. Across luxury, residential, commercial and hospitality, buyers and tenants increasingly buy a service architecture as much as a building. The awards have introduced explicit weighting on operational quality, service delivery and customer-experience integration within multiple category sets.

Together, these trends are widening the field of work eligible for recognition. Developers, architects, brokers and operators working at the intersection of these forces are increasingly central to the programme's most-watched categories.

Submitting Your Global Real Estate Awards Nomination

Nominations are submitted online via the official entry portal. Each category takes 20–30 minutes to complete and accepts written submissions, project images, drawings, videos and supporting evidence. Submissions are in English; supplementary local-language evidence is welcomed.

Once submitted, entries are acknowledged by the awards secretariat and progress through eligibility review, jury scoring, shortlisting and final winner announcement. Submit at least two weeks before the published deadline to allow time for any clarifications.

Detailed entry resources, sample entries and category criteria are published throughout this site. The awards team can be contacted directly for entry consultation, particularly for first-time entrants or organisations entering multiple categories. The Global Real Estate Awards gala is held annually in Dubai, bringing together the world's leading developers, architects, brokers, agencies, investors and property brands.
