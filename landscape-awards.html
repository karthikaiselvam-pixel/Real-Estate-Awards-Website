What The Landscape Awards Recognise

The Landscape Awards honour landscape architectural design — the discipline that increasingly defines how communities, properties and cities are experienced. The category set spans Residential Landscape Of The Year, Commercial Landscape Of The Year, Hospitality Landscape Of The Year, Urban Landscape Of The Year, Master-Plan Landscape Of The Year and Villa Landscape Of The Year.

Recognition matters in landscape because the landscape layer of a property is increasingly understood as a primary value driver — particularly in luxury residential, hospitality, commercial public-realm and master-plan property. Independent third-party recognition is a meaningful signal in landscape competitions, major commissions and HNW client engagement.

The 2026 programme spans every region. The landscape jury includes leading landscape architects, urbanists, ecological specialists and adjacent design professionals.

Why Landscape Designers Compete For Recognition

Landscape recognition supports practice credibility in international competitions and major commissions. For developers using landscape as differentiator, the recognition supports premium positioning. For luxury residential and hospitality operators, landscape recognition supports brand authority and customer-experience signalling.

Past Landscape Architecture Award winners have consistently reported stronger commission flow and accelerated developer-partnership conversations in the period following the award.

How To Win A Landscape Award

  • Lead with the landscape's distinctive idea and contextual response.
  • Address ecological integrity — biodiversity, native planting, soil and water strategy.
  • Address customer experience — how users experience the landscape across seasons.
  • Submit high-quality completed photography, drawings and growth-stage documentation.
  • Address operational maintenance — how the landscape lives over time.
  • Submit independent press coverage and verified ecological assessments where available.
