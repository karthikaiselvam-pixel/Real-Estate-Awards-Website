What The Property Awards Recognise

The Property Awards recognise exceptional work across the full property industry — from master developers shaping new cities to construction firms delivering technical excellence, from architects redefining the built environment to brokers and agencies driving sales performance. The programme is structured to honour both single-project excellence and sustained organisational performance.

The 2026 Property Awards span 35+ categories, five regions and 100+ countries. Each category is judged against published criteria by an independent jury of senior global experts. There are no closed-door decisions — scoring methodology, weightings and shortlist criteria are all published in advance.

Recognition matters because property is a credibility industry. Buyers, investors, lenders, partners and talent all read third-party recognition as a primary signal of organisational quality. The Property Awards function as that signal — across marketing, sales, PR, investor relations, partnership development and talent attraction.

Who Enters The Property Awards?

The programme is open to every organisation operating across the property industry. The most common entrant profile includes residential developers, commercial developers, mixed-use developers, master developers, luxury developers, sustainable developers, real estate agencies, brokerage networks, architectural practices, interior designers, construction firms, smart-building specialists, property managers and proptech innovators.

Sub-tracks within each category allow boutique organisations to compete fairly with larger international brands. A boutique residential developer in Lisbon, for example, is judged against comparable boutique residential developers across the world — not against global publicly-listed developers with hundreds of projects. The result is a programme that produces credible recognition across every scale of property business.

Property Award Categories

Project-level categories include Property Of The Year, Luxury Property Of The Year, Branded Residence Of The Year, Architecture Excellence (multiple sub-categories), Interior Design Of The Year, Landscape Design Of The Year, Smart Building Of The Year, Sustainable Property Of The Year and Adaptive Reuse Project Of The Year. Each is judged against published criteria specific to the category.

Why Property Brands Compete For Recognition

Property is one of the most credibility-driven industries in the world. Buyers spend millions; investors commit hundreds of millions; lenders price billions of dollars of risk; tenants commit to long leases — and every one of these decisions rests on whether the organisation can be trusted. Independent third-party recognition is a major credibility input across each of these channels.

For property developers raising institutional capital, an independent global award functions as a credibility multiplier with global LPs. For property brokers and agencies selling to HNW buyers, the award seal is a meaningful conversion lever. For property managers competing for institutional mandates, recognition is a meaningful tender-stage differentiator.

Past winners consistently report stronger inbound demand, faster sales cycles, improved competition shortlist rates and stronger inbound recruitment. The Property Awards are followed closely by global property press, institutional investors, family offices and HNW concierge platforms.

Entry Approach And Submission

Strong entries share a common architecture: clearly defined scope, evidence-led narrative, measurable outcomes and a presentation of work that allows the jury to assess design, performance and impact without ambiguity. The entry framework is intentionally lean — most submissions can be prepared in two to three working days.

Most entrants compete in two to four categories — typically a mix of corporate-level recognition (Property Developer Of The Year, Property Agency Of The Year) and project-level recognition (Property Of The Year, Luxury Property Of The Year). Multi-category entries qualify for discounted fees.

  • Lead with the project's or organisation's most distinctive idea or quality.
  • Quantify outcomes — sales, occupancy, certification, customer satisfaction, financial performance.
  • Show the design — high-quality photography, drawings, masterplans, renderings.
  • Submit independent press and third-party data as supporting evidence.
  • Submit two weeks before the published deadline.

The Property Awards Jury

The Property Awards jury includes senior global property developers, master architects, real estate fund executives, urban planners and academic experts. Jurors declare conflicts and recuse themselves where independence cannot be guaranteed. The jury chair publishes a yearly methodology note alongside the shortlist announcement, ensuring full transparency on scoring approach and weightings.

Submitting Your Property Awards Nomination

Nominations are submitted online via the official entry portal. Each category takes 20–30 minutes to complete and accepts written submissions, project images, drawings, videos and supporting evidence. Detailed entry resources, sample entries and category criteria are published throughout this site. The Property Awards gala is held annually in Dubai as part of the wider World Real Estate Excellence Awards programme.
